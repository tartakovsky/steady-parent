# Easy Homemade Presents to Make With Kids

**Source:** https://www.peacefulparenthappykids.com/read/homemade-presents

## Core Philosophy: The Value of Homemade Gifts

**"If it's the thought that counts, then what matters about a present is the love that goes into it."** This principle reframes gift-giving away from monetary value toward emotional investment. There's no need to spend a fortune on gifts for grandparents, cousins, friends, or teachers.

The homemade approach serves dual purposes:
1. Kids enjoy the making process itself
2. Recipients treasure the gifts because of their personal nature

## Mindset Shift: Releasing Perfectionism

**Fair warning:** If kids help, gifts are bound to look less professional. This is explicitly framed as a feature, not a bug - the imperfection makes gifts more valued by recipients, not less.

**The perfectionism trap to avoid:** Stop worrying about making gifts look professional. The goal is to have fun with your child and give a token of affection, not to exhaust yourself by showing off perfection.

**Reframe on worth:** "We all know you're more than enough, just the way you are. You don't need to show us how terrific you are with perfectly made gifts!" Any parent who makes presents with their kids is by definition terrific.

**Practical tip for grandparent gifts:** Take photos of your child making the present. Grandma will think it's priceless - the process documentation adds value beyond the finished product.

## Efficient Batch Production Strategy

Rather than crafting individual unique gifts, the recommended approach is to **"whip up big batches of something most folks will like"** - examples given are fudge or bath salts. This allows finishing all presents in one weekend while having fun with kids.

This strategy optimizes for:
- Time efficiency (batch production)
- Kid engagement (one concentrated fun session)
- Universal appeal (choose crowd-pleasers)

## 13 Homemade Gift Ideas

### 1. Booklet of Favorite Memories

Have child draw illustrations and dictate favorite memories of/with the recipient. Combine with photos in a binder to make a special gift book.

**Critical instruction:** Resist the urge to correct misspellings or funny comments - that's part of what makes this gift endearing. The imperfections carry authenticity and charm.

### 2. Kitchen Gifts

Options include:
- Cookies, jams, fudge, quick breads
- Your famous spaghetti or barbecue sauce
- Your special trail mix
- A kit with makings for something (perfect pancakes, seven bean soup) tied with ribbon

**Personalization element:** Have your child draw a personalized label.

### 3. Personalized Cookbook

Your recipes combined with your child's comments and drawings, assembled in a binder.

**To make it fancier:** Include family photos paired with recipes. Example given: accompany Aunt Ann's famous cornbread recipe with her photo. This connects recipes to family identity and memory.

### 4. Thank You Cards

Have kids dictate thank you messages detailing appreciation to everyone whose presence enriches their life all year long.

This reframes holiday gifts as an opportunity for gratitude practice, not just material exchange.

### 5. Handmade Bath Salts

**Recipe:** Mix Epsom salts or Kosher salt with essential oils, fragrance, and food coloring.

**Packaging:** Pour into recycled jars. Print a nice label, or better yet, have your child draw one (reinforcing the personalization-over-professional-appearance theme).

### 6. Candles

**Dipped candles method:** Add crayon chips to plain paraffin wax and dip wicks to make hand-dipped candles.

**Simpler alternative:** Let kids decorate store-bought candles. This acknowledges varying levels of ambition/time available.

### 7. Picture Frames

Decoration options: glitter, paint, glue-on shells.

**Key addition:** Add a photo of the artist. The gift becomes both functional and personal.

### 8. Homemade Calendars

Feature photos of the kids. Practical gift that keeps giving throughout the year with monthly kid photos.

### 9. Tie-Dyed Items

Teeshirts, sheets, etc. Instructions and inspiration available online for colorful tie-dying.

### 10. Canvas Bags

Two methods offered:
1. Indelible cloth markers (tip: work on a cookie sheet to contain the markers)
2. Iron-on designs

### 11. Art for Grandparents

**"For grandparents, a framed picture drawn by a grandchild is the perfect present."**

**Important detail:** Make sure child adds the date and their age. This transforms temporary kid art into a dated keepsake with developmental context.

**Alternative:** Coffee mugs with kids' drawings - described as something that "will be used and treasured for years." This addresses the practical longevity concern while maintaining the personal art element.

### 12. Gifts for Friends and Cousins

The possibilities listed: bubbles, homemade clay, finger paints, puppets, a kit of dress-up items, a kit of cool art supplies.

**Elaborate option:** A book of photos in a three ring binder with laminated pages that you and your kids create together. Content: details how much your child loves their cousins and all the things they do together. This is a relationship documentation gift.

### 13. Certificates for Services

This category spans multiple relationship types:

**Child-to-child:** Certificates for activities together

**Child-to-adult:** Certificates to help out by walking a dog or cleaning out the garage

**Parent-to-child:** "All children love certificates from parents" for:
- Helping them clean or paint their room
- Building a tree fort together
- A special excursion

This final category costs nothing material but promises time and experience - often the most valued gift type.
