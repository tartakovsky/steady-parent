# Parenting Twins: Managing Guilt and Self-Compassion

Source: https://www.peacefulparenthappykids.com/read/twins-how-to-be-physically-and-emotionally-available-to-two-babies-at-once

---

## The Core Challenge

Parents of twins often struggle with guilt about whether they were able to be sufficiently available - both physically and emotionally - to two babies simultaneously during the early years. This reflection can bring feelings of guilt and wistfulness.

## Key Insight: The Impossibility of Perfection

**No parent can be perfect for any child, and this is especially true with twins.** The fundamental reality is:

- If one baby is really hard, two babies at once is exponentially harder
- Being fully available to two infants simultaneously is not humanly possible
- Different families experience this challenge differently - some find it manageable, others find it extremely difficult

## The Path Forward: Self-Forgiveness

### Accept Human Limitations

The source emphasizes that parental growth often happens through raising children, which means parents inevitably "learn on" their children. This is described as part of the human condition rather than a personal failing.

### Reframe the Narrative

Rather than viewing past limitations as failures, consider:

- Growth and learning are ongoing processes for parents too
- The struggle itself can model important qualities: courage, forgiveness, and perseverance in the face of difficulty
- Creating meaning and love despite challenges is a valuable life skill to demonstrate

### Practice Self-Compassion

**Core recommendation: "Let go of the past, and commit yourself to loving now - beginning with yourself and your children."**

The emphasis is on:
1. Forgiving yourself for the ways you could not meet every need
2. Recognizing that self-forgiveness is itself a gift to your children
3. Modeling how to be human - including how to handle imperfection

## Key Principles Summary

1. Parenting twins is genuinely, objectively difficult - your struggles were real and valid
2. Perfect parenting is impossible for anyone, with any number of children
3. Self-forgiveness is not just okay but essential
4. How you handle imperfection and past regret teaches your children valuable lessons
5. The focus should shift from past guilt to present love and connection
