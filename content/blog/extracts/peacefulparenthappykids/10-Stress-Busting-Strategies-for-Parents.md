# Ten Stress-Busting Strategies for Parents

Source: https://www.peacefulparenthappykids.com/read/10-Stress-Busting-Strategies-for-Parents

---

## Core Insight: Stress and Parenting Quality Are Incompatible

The fundamental premise is that **you cannot be a good parent when you're stressed out**, regardless of how positive your intentions are. The opening quote captures this perfectly: "I'm stressed, we're rushing, and before I know it, I'm yelling. When I see the look on his face, I feel awful. He was just being a kid. And I was just stressed out."

This matters because your children are also stressed, and they take their signals from you. Their ability to cope in the moment depends at least partly on your ability to cope. The goal should be to give your kids the best of you, not what's left of you.

## The Nature of Stress

Stress has two components:

1. **What happens to us** - external events and circumstances
2. **Our reaction to it** - our internal response

The critical insight is that the same things that stress one person may just roll off the back of another. This means stress is at least partly within our control. We cannot control what happens to us, but we can - at least sometimes - control our response.

Each parent has the responsibility to manage their own stress because it directly affects their children's wellbeing.

## The Three-Pronged Framework for Managing Stress

A comprehensive approach to stress requires working on all three levels simultaneously:

1. **Pare down the stressors** - Reduce the actual sources of stress in your life
2. **Keep your cup full** - Build internal resources so you have more capacity to deal with life's inevitable curve balls
3. **Re-train your attitude** - Develop awareness to notice stress building and consciously choose to shift gears

The ten strategies below draw on all three of these approaches.

## Strategy 1: Conscious Breathing Throughout the Day

### The Problem with Automatic Breathing

Breathing is automatic, which means we don't even notice when stress causes our breathing to become shallow. Shallow breathing then escalates the stress cycle, creating a feedback loop.

### Why Conscious Deep Breathing Works

**Biochemical effect:** Taking deep breaths delivers more oxygen to your body, which actually reduces the stress hormones circulating in your bloodstream.

**Pause button effect:** Noticing your breath brings you back into the present moment, giving you a choice about how to respond. Without this pause, you risk getting hijacked by stress and ending up yelling at your child when you could choose a better way to handle things.

**Body-emotion connection:** Bringing conscious awareness to your breath connects you more fully to your entire body. Since the body is where you process emotion, this helps you process emotions as they arise, which decreases your overall stress level.

### Practical Application

When stress-inducing moments happen - late for an appointment, kids dump toys in the toilet - the response is simple: breathe.

**Implementation tip:** Put post-it notes all over your house and in your car as reminders. When you see one, take 3 deep breaths. This regular practice makes your attempts to breathe calmly more effective when you actually face stress-inducing moments.

## Strategy 2: Pare Down Your Schedule

### Why Over-Scheduling Creates Stress

Much of parenting stress comes from **routinely over-scheduling**. This is a choice we make. We think squeezing more in is helpful, but it always costs us.

### The Connection to Child Behavior

Children thrive on connection. When parents get too busy to just hang out and connect with their kids, the children act out. This acting out then creates more stress, when the root cause was the over-scheduling in the first place.

## Strategy 3: Sleep

### Why Sleep Is Essential for Stress Management

Your body uses sleep time to heal and restore a better biochemical balance. Sleep is an essential stress buster - not optional.

### Addressing the "I Can't Control Sleep" Objection

Sleep often feels out of our control, especially when young children wake you up. However, you **can** get more sleep, even if it means going to bed when your kids do.

### Practical Recommendation

"Just do it." Even implementing this discipline a few days a week will ease anxiety (which is a form of stress) and lift your mood. The benefits extend to every aspect of physical health.

---

## Strategy 4: Move

### Exercise as a Top-Tier Stress Reducer

Exercise is the best stress reducer there is, after deep breathing and getting enough sleep.

### Specific Recommendation

**Twenty minutes of sweat-inducing activity every day** will cut cortisol levels and put you in a good mood.

### Making It Family-Friendly

Turn on music and get your kids dancing with you. This accomplishes two goals: you get your stress-reducing exercise, and you create connection with your children.

## Strategy 5: Cut Your Kid - and Yourself - Some Slack

### What You Don't Need to Stress About

You don't need to stress about every little misstep from your child. Specific examples: when Michael clobbered his sister or Jillian smeared poop on the wall - these don't mean they'll be psychopaths.

### The Developmental Perspective

Most childish behavior is **developmentally appropriate**. When you understand this, the behavior becomes less alarming and therefore less stressful.

### The "Good-Enough Parent" Standard

You don't have to be perfect to be a good-enough parent. The standard is not perfection.

### Reframing the Child's Behavior

When your child acts out, this is your baby showing you in the best way they can that they need your help and understanding. This reframe changes the situation from adversarial to collaborative.

### The Cure for Most Acting Out

Most acting out can be cured by reconnecting with your child and getting some real laughter going between you. This laughter heals both your stress.

### The Gratitude Anchor

On days when you're ready to tear your hair out, remember that moment when you first held your child in your arms and how lucky you felt. You're still that lucky, and you can still feel that way. Feeling lucky and grateful is a choice you can make.

## Strategy 6: Postpone Anything But Love

### When You Need to Set Limits

Parents do need to set limits and guide kids, all day every day. But there's a crucial insight about timing.

### The "Not an Emergency" Reframe

First, remind yourself that it's not an emergency. You don't have to teach your child a lesson this very moment.

### What You Actually Have to Do Right Now

In the moment of conflict, all you have to do is:
1. Keep everyone safe
2. Return yourself to calm

That's it. Nothing more is required immediately.

### Why Waiting Makes You More Effective

Anything you need to say to your child, you will say better once you're calm and able to reconnect with love.

### The Benefits of This Approach

**Reduces pressure and powerlessness:** This eliminates the stress of feeling so pressured and powerless in that moment to say the right thing (or come up with the right threat).

**Prevents regret:** It stops you from saying things you're sorry for later.

**Increases effectiveness:** This makes you a more effective parent because calm, connected communication is more impactful.

## Strategy 7: Give Up Your Perfectionism

### The Messy Reality

Life is messy. But is that any reason to postpone love and happiness? No.

### The Self-Acceptance Message

You're more than enough, just the way you are. There are no perfect people, and there are no perfect parents.

### Why Perfectionism Is Damaging

Trying to be perfect is a huge source of stress. It "tightens a vise around your heart."

### The Most Important Thing You Can Do

**Loving yourself unconditionally** - really feeling acceptance and compassion for yourself, even though you aren't perfect - may be the single most important thing you can do to help your child thrive.

The reasoning: You can only give what you have inside. If you don't have self-compassion inside, you cannot give compassion to your child.

### Practical Applications

**Practice positive self-talk** - Change your internal dialogue.

**Find ways to nurture yourself** - Active self-care.

**How to evaluate yourself as a parent:** If you must evaluate yourself, never judge yourself by your child's behavior, but by your reaction to it. Your child's behavior is not under your control; your reaction is.

### If Unconditional Love Feels Too Hard

If unconditional love sounds like too much of a stretch, take it day by day with the mantra: **"Only love today."**

## Strategy 8: Count Your Blessings and Cultivate Optimism

### The Gratitude Practice

Every time you start to feel negative, find as many things as you can to be grateful for, and really feel that gratitude. The emphasis on really feeling it is important - this isn't just a mental exercise.

### Evidence Base

Research shows that this practice reduces stress and improves health and happiness.

### Role Modeling for Children

If you talk about your gratitude practice as you do it, your kids will learn from your role modeling.

### Practical Implementation

Start a gratitude practice every evening at the dinner table. This creates routine, involves the family, and provides consistent practice.

## Strategy 9: Find Support

### The Core Truth About Parenting

Parenting is the hardest job there is. We ALL need support to parent well. No one can do it alone.

### Types of Support

**Parenting audios** that soothe and inspire you - auditory support for when you're overwhelmed.

**Other parents** to whom you can vent, who won't judge you or try to fix you. The key qualities are non-judgment and not trying to fix. Sometimes you just need to be heard.

**Parenting courses** that inspire you and include lots of ongoing support. The ongoing support element is emphasized.

**Journaling** - The simple act of writing in a journal develops your "observing ego" and has been proven to be an effective way of supporting yourself to grow and coming to peace with things that bother you.

---

## Strategy 10: Find Spirit in Your Life

### Broad Definition

This can mean a higher power, but it doesn't have to. The point is to step away from the distractions and find the quiet that inspires you to connect with your deepest wisdom.

### Simple Examples

For some, it's as simple as a walk in the woods or gazing at the stars.

### Benefits for Children Too

Your kids benefit from quiet time in nature as well. We all need to reconnect regularly with the miracles that make life worth living.

## Key Themes Across All Strategies

**Control what you can:** You cannot control external stressors or your child's behavior, but you can control your reactions, your schedule, your self-talk, and your choices about sleep, exercise, and breathing.

**The cup metaphor:** Keeping your "cup full" means having internal resources to draw upon when stress hits. When the cup is empty, there's nothing left to give.

**Children take cues from parents:** Your stress response teaches your children how to handle stress. Your calm teaches them calm.

**Connection heals:** Many strategies circle back to connection - with yourself, with your child, with other parents, with something larger than yourself.

**Not an emergency:** Most parenting moments that feel urgent are not actually emergencies. Slowing down and returning to calm first almost always produces better outcomes.

**Self-compassion enables child compassion:** You can only give what you have inside. Treating yourself with compassion is not selfish - it's necessary for being able to treat your child with compassion.
