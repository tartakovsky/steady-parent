# Tooth Development in One-Year-Olds

Source: Peaceful Parent Happy Kids - Dr. Laura Markham

## Normal Variation in Tooth Eruption Timing

**Having only two teeth at one year old is well within the normal range.** This applies to the specific case of a child with two bottom teeth and no signs of additional teeth emerging.

The key insight is that **tooth development varies significantly between individual children**. There is no single "correct" timeline for when teeth should appear.

### Range of Normal

- Some babies have only two teeth at their first birthday
- Some babies have no teeth at all on their first birthday
- Both scenarios fall within normal developmental variation

The variation is not a cause for concern in itself because every child develops at their own pace. The absence of teeth does not indicate a developmental problem.

## When to Seek Professional Guidance

While the variation described above is normal, **parents who are worried should consult their pediatrician**. This serves two purposes:

1. It provides professional reassurance based on examining the specific child
2. It rules out any rare underlying issues that might warrant attention

The recommendation to check with a pediatrician is not because something is likely wrong, but because parental peace of mind matters and professional evaluation is the appropriate response to parental concern.

## Key Takeaway

**Do not worry** if your one-year-old has few or no teeth. This is a normal variation in child development. Individual differences in tooth eruption timing are expected and do not signal problems. When in doubt, your pediatrician can provide personalized guidance.
