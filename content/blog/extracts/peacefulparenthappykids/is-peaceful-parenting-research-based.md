# Research Foundation of Peaceful Parenting

Source: Peaceful Parent Happy Kids - Dr. Laura Markham

---

## Core Claim

The Peaceful Parenting approach is not "soft" parenting without evidence. All child development research from the past several decades supports this approach. There is no research that contradicts it.

---

## The Three Pillars of Peaceful Parenting and Their Research Base

### 1. Connection (80% of the Approach)

**Attachment research** is now at the center of all child development theory because securely attached children perform better on every measure of emotional and physical health.

**Why connection matters**: Attachment depends on the parent's responsiveness to the child. Empathy is the mechanism through which responsiveness operates. This is why Dr. Laura states that Connection comprises 80% of her parenting approach - it is the foundational element that enables everything else.

**The causal chain**: Parent responsiveness -> Secure attachment -> Better outcomes across all emotional and physical health measures.

---

### 2. Coaching (Two Components)

#### A. Empathic Limits

**Diana Baumrind's research** (1960s-70s, replicated many times) established a critical distinction between parenting styles:

**Authoritative parenting** (the most effective style) involves:
- Setting limits
- Giving the child support to meet the parent's expectations
- NOT punishing the child for failing to meet expectations

This is contrasted with three less effective styles:
- **Permissive parenting**: Not setting sufficient limits
- **Authoritarian parenting**: Setting limits through punishment rather than support
- **Uninvolved parenting**: Neither setting limits nor providing support

**Key outcome**: Children raised with authoritative parenting succeed more often at school, with peers, and at home compared to children raised under any other parenting style.

**Why punishment backfires**: Alfie Kohn's book "Unconditional Parenting" assembles extensive research demonstrating why punishment is counterproductive. New studies continue to be published each year supporting this finding.

#### B. Emotion-Coaching

**John Gottman**, one of the world's leading researchers on family dynamics, established approximately two decades ago that emotion-coaching produces emotionally healthier children.

**What emotion-coaching is NOT** (approaches that produce worse outcomes):
- Shutting down children's emotions
- Distracting the child from their emotions
- Punishing children for having emotions

**The comparison**: Parents who emotion-coach raise children who are emotionally much healthier than parents who use any of the above three approaches.

---

### 3. Self-Regulation

**The research finding**: When parents self-regulate instead of yelling at their children, three positive outcomes emerge:
1. Children are healthier
2. Parents are healthier
3. The parent-child relationship is stronger

This establishes that parental self-regulation is not just about being "nice" - it produces measurable benefits for everyone involved.

---

## Addressing the "Too Soft" Critique

The context of this article is a parent of three adopted children being told by professionals that peaceful parenting is "too soft" and "not research-based."

**Dr. Laura's response** directly counters both claims:
1. The approach is thoroughly research-based (as detailed above)
2. Being responsive and supportive is not the same as being permissive - authoritative parenting explicitly includes setting limits, just with support rather than punishment

The distinction between "soft" (permissive) parenting and peaceful (authoritative) parenting is critical: peaceful parenting maintains boundaries while providing emotional support, which research shows is more effective than either pure permissiveness or punitive approaches.
