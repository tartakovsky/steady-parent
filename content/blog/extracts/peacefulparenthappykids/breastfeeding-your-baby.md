# Breastfeeding Tips

Source: https://www.peacefulparenthappykids.com/read/breastfeeding-your-baby

---

## Key Insights

### Latching On Fundamentals
- **Latching on** is the term for how the baby holds your areola with her mouth - proper latching ensures baby gets enough milk without causing nipple pain
- The **entire areola** (not just the nipple) should be inside the baby's mouth for correct nursing position
- If only the nipple is in the mouth, milk ducts won't receive enough stimulation and nipples will become sore

### Step-by-Step Breastfeeding Guide

1. **Get comfortable first** - In early hours after birth, sit up straight in bed with pillow support for back, arm, neck, or knees. Later you'll find other comfortable spots and eventually be able to breastfeed anywhere.

2. **Position baby correctly** - Place baby's head in elbow crook, support back and buttocks with forearm and hand. Baby should lie on their side facing you - never on their back having to turn head to feed (uncomfortable swallowing position).

3. **Initiate the rooting reflex** - With free hand, grasp breast with thumb on top and tickle baby's lips or cheek with nipple. This causes the rooting reflex in hungry babies.

4. **Wait for wide mouth opening** - Baby's mouth must be wide open before latching so most of the areola goes in. The tongue stimulates the bottom of the areola to express milk from the ducts.

5. **Press baby to you** - Don't lean forward to the baby. Ensure most of the areola is inside the mouth. You should see baby's tongue sticking out slightly between breast and lower lip. If positioning is wrong, use forefinger to gently break the seal and try again.

6. **Recognize proper feeding** - Feel the let-down reflex, see jaw movement and swallowing action. No nipple pain means correct positioning. Nurse at least 10-15 minutes on first side, then switch to second side until baby falls asleep and breast falls from mouth.

7. **Burp when needed** - Try shoulder position with gentle back patting, sitting up with head support, or tummy-down on lap. Some spit-up is normal. Most breastfed babies don't need to burp, but some do.

### Encouragement for New Mothers
- Before long, nursing will feel completely comfortable and natural
- The breastfeeding relationship between mother and infant gives humans the best start in life

## Practical Takeaways

- **Comfort is key**: Set up a supportive environment with pillows before beginning
- **Side-lying position for baby**: Never expect baby to turn head while on back
- **Wide mouth = success**: Wait for full mouth opening before latching
- **Areola not nipple**: Most of the areola should be in baby's mouth
- **Move baby to you**: Don't lean forward, bring baby toward your breast
- **Check for tongue visibility**: Visible tongue between breast and lower lip indicates correct latch
- **Don't hesitate to re-latch**: Breaking seal and trying again is normal and expected
- **Pain signals problems**: No pain during nursing means correct positioning
- **Individual burping needs vary**: Observe your baby to learn their pattern
