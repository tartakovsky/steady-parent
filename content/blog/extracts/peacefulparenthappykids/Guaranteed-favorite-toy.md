# Your Child's Guaranteed Favorite Toy: Connection Over Materialism

Source: Peaceful Parent Happy Kids (Dr. Laura Markham)

## Core Insight: Why Material Gifts Fall Short

Material presents work like drugs: the dopamine lift is temporary, followed by a deeper inner craving. This is human biology designed to keep us motivated toward survival goals. When this boom and bust cycle repeats quickly, especially during holidays, it becomes tinged with desperation.

**The psychological component**: Focusing on presents fuels the fantasy that material things can give us what we need inside. This does not work for long, leading to a constant search for the next "thing" and making it hard to appreciate what we have.

**The truth**: Your child IS enough and has enough. Happiness comes from connection, meaning, and contribution, not from things.


## Why Words Are Not Enough

Telling children they are enough is important, but you must LIVE it for children to believe it. You do this by taking the focus off presents and putting it back on the love between you and your child.

**Key principle**: Children spell love TIME.


## Four Strategies to Shift Focus to Connection

### 1. Give the Gift of Your Time

**Research finding**: When children were asked what they liked best about vacations, it was not fancy vacations or time off from school. It was simple connection time with parents when the parent could relax and enjoy the child without being stressed.

**Children reported things like**: "Lying on the blanket with my Dad, looking at the stars together."

**Why time gifts work better**: Instead of feeding the dopamine craving cycle, these gifts give children lasting pleasure from:
- The anticipation
- The loving connection
- The savoring afterwards

**How to implement**:
1. Brainstorm activities with you that your child will enjoy
2. Print out certificates and wrap them so your child gets the pleasure of opening a present
3. Turn off your phone during these activities

**Example activity certificates**:
- Go on a hike and picnic with the parent of your choice
- Dad's best foot massage
- Paint your room with Mom
- Soccer session with Dad
- Ice cream date with a parent
- Mom's special manicure
- Choose the movie for family movie night


### 2. Share the Magic of the Holiday

**For Hanukkah**: Take time with the lights off to sit in silence with your child to enjoy the flames.

**For Christmas**: Let your child fall asleep in front of your tree in a sleeping bag with you by their side, chatting softly. Consider having a slumber party, one child at a time, sleeping on the couch next to your child. This will likely become your child's favorite holiday memory.

**For any holiday**: Go for a quiet walk together to look at neighborhood lights.

**Why walks work**:
- Fresh air melts away stress so everyone sleeps better
- The dark night inspires awe
- The novelty and sense of adventure create connection
- The walk gives a nice chance to chat, away from screens

**Important**: Turn off all cell phones and just enjoy the moment with your family.


### 3. Shift the Focus to Connection and Giving

**Reframe the Santa tradition**: The magic is about Giving, not about photos on a bearded stranger's lap reciting lists of material items they covet.

**Better traditions focused on connection and giving**:
- Take photos together making Grandma's present
- Take photos decorating for the holiday
- Take photos baking treats for their class party
- Send photos to Grandma with her present
- Let your child glue copies of photos onto paper to make a little book about family holidays

**Why the photo book matters**: Children often ask for this book over and over, or read it to comfort themselves.


### 4. Start a Blessing Bowl for Gratitude

**How it works**:
1. Over the course of the next month, write down things you and other family members are grateful for from your year
2. Toss them into a Blessing Bowl as you think about them
3. Ask children to contribute too
4. One evening towards the end of the year, make popcorn, snuggle up together, and go through the bowl reading each gratitude item aloud
5. Talk about them together
6. Savor the feeling of emotional abundance that makes you feel grateful

**What to share**: How blessed you feel your family is, particularly to have each other.

**Important**: Resist the urge to lecture.

**Research backing**: This creates long-lasting feelings of gratitude, connection, and meaning, which research shows makes kids and adults happier even months later.


## The Bottom Line

There is nothing wrong with exchanging presents to express your delight in each other. But keeping the focus on connection and meaning fills your child inside in a way that objects can never do. THAT is what makes dreams come true.
