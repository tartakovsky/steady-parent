# Practical Mindfulness Tips to Shift Your Mood

Source: https://www.peacefulparenthappykids.com/read/Notice-how-your-mind-determines-your-moods

---

## Core Premise: The Mind-Mood Connection

You cannot stop your mind from being negative sometimes. However, you can develop a better way of handling **low-level fear** when it arises, before it spirals out of control. The key is noticing the creeping negativity early and applying practical mindfulness techniques to shift your mood.

---

## The Seven-Step Framework for Shifting Mood

### Step 1: Use Your Pause Button

**The "Stop, Drop, and Breathe" Technique**

Train yourself to apply this three-part response the minute you notice something is stressing you:

1. **Stop** - Interrupt whatever you're doing
2. **Drop** - Let go of your current agenda, just for now
3. **Breathe** - Take three deep conscious breaths

**Why this works:** Three deep conscious breaths will pause your stress response. This gives you the power to choose how to react to whatever is in front of you, instead of getting hijacked by your emotions. The pause creates space between stimulus and response.

---

### Step 2: Observe What's Happening in Your Body

**Why body observation beats mental rumination:** Ruminating about whatever is upsetting you will just get you stuck in the story line. Mental analysis keeps you trapped in the narrative. Body awareness creates a different kind of engagement with the emotion.

**What to notice:** Pay attention to physical sensations - tight shoulders, dread in the pit of your stomach, or other tension patterns.

**The key practice:** Be willing to feel those sensations. The secret is that as you welcome those sensations and breathe into them, the feelings begin to shift, change, and eventually evaporate. Welcoming rather than resisting is essential.

**When you still feel upset:** This likely means you're bumping up against a **powerful belief system** that you've reinforced with your thoughts many times. It has worn a groove in your psyche and triggers big feelings.

**The excavation process:** Every time you:
- Notice those automatic negative thoughts
- Feel the sensations that go with them
- BUT - and this is crucial - **resist taking action** (which is a distraction from the feelings)

...you break that neural chain. Eventually, you won't even get triggered by those thoughts, and that belief system will no longer feel true.

**Critical warning:** Taking action serves as a distraction from feelings. The urge to "do something" often prevents the emotional processing that would actually resolve the issue. Sitting with discomfort without acting breaks the automatic response pattern.

---

### Step 3: Make Your Conscious Attention Bigger Than Your Fear

This is described as a **"power tool"** to accelerate the process while you're still upset.

**How to be more present:**
- Shift your attention from your mind back into your body
- Breathe deeply
- Notice everything your senses are telling you

**The mechanism:** Notice that as you observe, the part of you that's observing gets bigger than those uncomfortable sensations. Your conscious attention is like a light that melts away tension and fear.

**The key insight:** Observation itself transforms the experience. The observer self expands beyond the fear when engaged in genuine presence.

---

### Step 4: Open Space to Shift Out of Feeling Victimized

**Understanding fear:** Fear makes us feel powerless and victimized. But what IS fear, really? It's just the worry that we won't be able to handle something that might happen. Fear is anticipatory and hypothetical by nature.

**The antidote - claiming your power:** Once you've spent some time in your body dissolving those stuck patterns, shift the energy through active techniques.

**Three specific techniques:**

1. **Tapping with affirmation:** Tap the karate chop point on the side of your hand as you say: "Even though I'm feeling stressed about that right now, I know that whatever happens, I can handle it!"

2. **Physical release:** Shake the tension out of your hands or run them under water, imagining that you're off-loading fear.

3. **Movement:** Put on some music and dance it out, claiming your power. (This is noted as terrific to do with children - making it a family practice.)

**Sequence matters:** These active techniques work best AFTER you've done the body awareness work in earlier steps. The sequence is: pause -> feel -> observe -> then actively release.

---

### Step 5: Embrace Yourself with Compassion

**The self-compassion principle:** Whatever you're going through that feels hard to you, acknowledge that it's hard. You don't need to compare yourself to anyone else. If it's hard, it's hard.

**Why this matters:** Taking action to comfort yourself and help yourself feel safer allows your body to offload stress. Safety signals reduce the stress response.

**The friend standard:** Give yourself all the compassion you would offer a good friend who was struggling. This external perspective helps overcome the tendency to be harsh with ourselves.

**Three specific self-compassion practices:**

1. **Physical self-embrace:** Wrap your arms around yourself and hug yourself firmly.

2. **Mirror work with safety affirmation:** Look at yourself lovingly in the mirror and tell yourself you're safe.

3. **Compassion breathing:** Sit quietly and send yourself compassion with each in-breath. With each out-breath, breathe out stress.

---

### Step 6: Change Your Mind

**Cognitive reframing techniques:**

1. **Question the truth of upsetting thoughts:** Notice that the upsetting thoughts causing your unhappy feelings may not even be true. Reframe mentally with a thought that is more positive and just as likely to be true.
   - **Example:** "Every baby sleeps through the night sooner or later." (This shows how to find an equally plausible positive interpretation of a stressful situation.)

2. **Use a mantra:** Talk yourself down and reframe your perceptions so your mind turns off its alarm system. Repetition of calming phrases interrupts the anxiety cycle.

3. **Perspective-taking:** Choose to see things from the other person's perspective. This shifts you out of single-track thinking.

4. **Balance with appreciation:** Remind yourself of all the wonderful things about your child to put any current "offense" into perspective. Context reduces the perceived magnitude of problems.

5. **Choose a more expansive emotion - particularly gratitude:** The simple act of choosing to feel grateful:
   - Stills our inner critic
   - Changes our body chemistry
   - Floods us with appreciation and love

**Jan Marie Dorr's gratitude framework:** "Practice being grateful for what you have, rather than focusing on what's missing. Look at what's working in your life rather than what's not working. Practice being grateful for the small, wonderful things that happen to you every day, and this will automatically shift your attention from problems to joy, from stress to inner peace."

---

### Step 7: Change Your Environment

**When to take this step:** Still upset because something in your world really isn't working for you? This step is for when internal work alone isn't sufficient because external circumstances genuinely need to change.

**Critical prerequisite:** Start by doing the first five steps above to let go of where you're stuck, empower yourself, and shift into a more positive place.

**Warning - the fear-action trap:** Actions you take from fear won't get you to a better place. This is why the internal work must come first. Acting from fear perpetuates fear-based patterns.

**The love-centered approach:**

1. **Consciously choose love** for yourself and everyone in the situation

2. **Visualization:** Imagine the situation working out for everyone's highest good. Everyone wins. See how that loosens the situation. This "everyone wins" framing opens creative solutions that adversarial thinking cannot access.

3. **Clarity emerges from the work:** Now that you've done this work, you'll have more clarity about how to get where you want to go.

4. **Take incremental action:** Take one step toward a better future. Just one. See what happens. Small steps allow course correction and prevent overwhelm.

## The Deeper Purpose

This is described as the hardest work we can do. You're bringing the light of your conscious awareness to your fears, so you can spin them into the gold of love.

This is suggested as potentially a big part of our life-purpose. However, you don't have to believe that to realize the transformative effects. The practical benefits exist regardless of whether you adopt the philosophical framing.

---

## Key Mechanisms and Principles

**Neural pattern breaking:** The combination of awareness + feeling + non-action breaks established neural chains tied to belief systems.

**Observer effect:** Conscious attention expands to become larger than fear, effectively dissolving it.

**Body as processing center:** The body, not the mind, is where emotional processing happens. Mental rumination keeps you stuck; body awareness enables transformation.

**Sequence dependency:** The steps build on each other. Active release works after body awareness. Environmental change works after internal shifts. Actions from calm differ qualitatively from actions from fear.

**Welcoming vs. resisting:** Welcoming uncomfortable sensations allows them to shift and evaporate. Resisting them (including through action) keeps them stuck.
