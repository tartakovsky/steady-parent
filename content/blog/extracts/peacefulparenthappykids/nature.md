# How Nature Makes Kids Calmer, Healthier, Smarter

Source: Peaceful Parent Happy Kids - Dr. Laura Markham

---

## The Core Problem: Nature Deprivation in Modern Childhood

The average modern child spends most of their time indoors, fundamentally disconnected from outdoor experience. This happens due to several converging factors:

1. **Lack of outdoor space** - Many families no longer have yards
2. **No neighborhood playmates** - Even when yards exist, there are often no other children outside to play with
3. **Safety concerns requiring supervision** - Parents feel they must stay outside with children, but have other responsibilities like making dinner
4. **Competing indoor entertainment** - The result is that the average fifth grader, given a choice, prefers to stay inside near electrical outlets and the entertainment devices they power

Richard Louv captures a key generational shift: parents used to be outdoors naturally, not necessarily exercising but simply present outside, on porches, talking to neighbors. Today's parents may go jogging, but kids are not outside. This means children have lost both the habit and the social context for outdoor time.

**The consequence**: "Today's kids are the sorriest generation in the history of the United States" in terms of physical fitness, according to Louv.

## The Seven Research-Backed Benefits of Nature for Children

### 1. Calmer Nervous Systems

Fresh air and nature soothe the nervous systems of both children and adults. This benefit is particularly important for children with ADHD because time in nature actually lowers their need for medication. The calming effect is physiological, not just psychological.

### 2. Improved Mood and Happiness

Three distinct factors combine to encourage good moods and reduce tendencies toward depression:
- Sunshine
- Fresh air
- Physical activity

Studies show each of these independently contributes to mental wellbeing.

### 3. Better Physical Health

Multiple health pathways are involved:

**Vitamin D deficiency** - Many indoor children lack adequate Vitamin D, which affects both physical health and mood

**Air quality** - Indoor air is usually less healthy than outdoor air

**Stress reduction** - Simply walking in nature decreases stress hormones and blood pressure. This is a measurable physiological effect, not merely subjective feeling.

### 4. Protection Against Obesity

Pediatricians recommend at least one hour of active physical play daily during childhood specifically to protect against obesity and diabetes. Outdoor play naturally provides this active movement in ways that indoor activities typically do not.

### 5. Better Vision

Children who play outdoors more have better vision and less need for eyeglasses. Multiple mechanisms explain this:

- **Reduced screen time** means less close-focus strain
- **Distance focusing** exercises the eyes differently when looking at far objects outdoors
- **Vitamin D** plays a role in eye health
- **Sunlight exposure** itself is important for healthy eye function, according to the latest research

### 6. Academic Performance

Research shows outdoor play produces:
- Longer attention spans
- More frustration tolerance
- Better school performance
- Better test scores when children are allowed to play first

**The mechanism is not just "getting wiggles out"** - it's the oxygen to the brain that comes from physical activity outdoors that enhances cognitive function.

### 7. Enhanced Creativity

Outdoor play is typically less structured than indoor activities with technology. This lack of structure means children exercise their imaginations along with their bodies. The open-ended nature of outdoor environments invites creative thinking.

## The Solution Framework: Parents Must Go Outside Too

The fundamental insight is that children generally cannot be outdoors without parents in modern contexts. Therefore, the answer is not to send kids outside, but to set up family life so both parents and children are outdoors more often.

This requires two approaches working together:

### Approach 1: Make Your Outdoor Space Inviting

Transform whatever outdoor access you have into a draw for children:

**Permanent structures** (if possible):
- Sandbox
- Wading pool
- Swing
- Climbing structure
- Garden

**Impermanent alternatives** (always possible):
- Tablecloth teepee
- Bucket of water with funnels and cups
- Shovel to dig a hole (that can later be refilled)

The key is spending time outside WITH your child in these spaces.

### Approach 2: Family Nature Time Together

Spend time as a family in nature through activities like:
- Hiking
- Playing tag
- Biking
- Simply walking together in a beautiful place

**Why this matters beyond physical benefits:**
- Allows the family to regroup and get back in sync
- Creates wonderful lasting memories
- Provides a great workout for everyone

**Important**: This does not have to be a big production. If you have a yard, options range from kicking a ball to camping in a tent. Every city has public parks, and every family can find something fun to do outside.

## Two Critical Ground Rules for Family Outdoor Time

### Rule 1: Turn Off Cell Phones

Turn off YOUR phone. Turn off THEIR phones. Really.

**The reasoning:**
- The world will be waiting when you get back
- This is quality time meant for family focus
- Your child will soon enough prefer friends over you
- This time to connect is limited and precious
- You must be a role model demonstrating what you value

### Rule 2: Minimize Competition in Favor of Fun

If you choose to engage in sports:
- Relax the rules for little ones
- Prioritize everyone enjoying themselves
- The goal is fun, not winning

## Ten Specific Activity Ideas

### 1. Park Picnic Play
Take a blanket, snacks, and a ball or frisbee to the park. Play kickball, soccer, or catch. Blow bubbles. Play tag.

### 2. Family Bike Rides
This activity improves as kids get older. When children are young, tandem bikes can be rented to make it work.

### 3. Nature Walks

**Key activities:**
- Collect rocks or leaves
- Look for animal footprints
- Watch bugs
- Soak up smells, sounds, and sights

**Critical guidance**: Simply experiencing nature is sufficient. You do not need to take your child out of their heart and into their head by giving science lectures. The experience of watching a butterfly is more transformative than reading about it.

**When questions arise**: By all means follow the child's natural curiosity. Help them look up answers when they get home. But let the direct experience lead.

### 4. Scavenger Hunts

Keep items simple:
- Something red
- Something tiny
- Something that moves
- Something bigger than your hand
- Something that bends
- Something beautiful

**Important**: Make sure everyone wins. This is about engagement, not competition.

### 5. Water Play

Give kids water and they will find ways to play with it. Invite neighborhood kids over for a water party. Set up the sprinkler and cut up watermelon.

### 6. Gardening

Even a window box provides the opportunity to garden with your child. There is magic in:
- Planting seeds and watching them sprout into plants
- Eating lettuce that you grew yourself

The transformation from seed to food is inherently engaging for children.

### 7. Bird Feeding

Make a bird feeder and place it where you can observe visitors. This creates ongoing engagement with wildlife from your home.

### 8. Sunset Watching

The author's children used to say "Mom, let's watch the show!" The family would gather on a hill to watch the sun go down and clap for it. The moment when the sun goes below the horizon is always magical, even when there are no spectacular colors.

### 9. Night Walks

Bring flashlights for fun and safety, but be sure to turn them off for:
- Listening to nature sounds
- Star gazing

The experience changes entirely when artificial light is removed.

### 10. Campfire Time

If you have a safe place for a campfire, use it for:
- Telling stories of when you were a kid
- Sharing what your children did when they were younger
- Singing songs
- Roasting marshmallows and making s'mores
- Simply relaxing in silence and watching the fire

**Opportunity for skill-building**: Children who want to build the fire can be taught fire safety and given responsibility with matches under supervision. This channels fascination productively.

---

## The Deeper Value

These outdoor experiences become the memories children treasure as they get older. Beyond physical and cognitive benefits, nature provides every child connection to the essence of life. You are feeding your child's soul as well as their body.
