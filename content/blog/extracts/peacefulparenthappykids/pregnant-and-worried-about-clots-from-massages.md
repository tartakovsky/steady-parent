# Massage Safety During Pregnancy: Blood Clot Concerns

Source: Peaceful Parent Happy Kids - Q&A Response

---

## The Core Concern

A common worry among pregnant couples is whether foot massage can release a blood clot. This fear often comes from secondhand information passed between parents.

## Key Distinction: Existing Clots vs. General Massage

The safety of massage during pregnancy depends entirely on whether a blood clot is already present:

**If you suspect a blood clot exists:** Do NOT massage the area. The reasoning is that massaging an existing clot could dislodge it, potentially causing it to travel to the lungs, which is a serious medical emergency. This applies whether or not you are pregnant.

**If you do not have a blood clot:** Massage during pregnancy offers multiple benefits. These include reducing stress on weight-bearing joints, with the feet being a particular area that benefits due to the extra weight carried during pregnancy.

## What To Do If You Suspect a Blood Clot

1. Look up symptoms of blood clots online to compare against what you're experiencing
2. If symptoms match, contact your doctor immediately
3. Do not massage the suspected area

## Benefits of Pregnancy Massage (When No Clot Is Present)

- Reduces stress on weight-bearing joints
- Provides relief for feet that bear extra pregnancy weight
- Can be done regularly (the author had weekly massages during pregnancy with no issues)

## Important Caveat

This guidance comes from a psychologist, not a medical doctor. For specific medical concerns about blood clots during pregnancy, consult with a healthcare provider who can assess your individual situation.

## The Bottom Line

The fear of "releasing a clot" through massage misunderstands the actual risk. Massage does not create clots. The concern only applies when a clot already exists. For pregnant women without blood clot symptoms, massage remains a beneficial practice for managing the physical demands of pregnancy.
