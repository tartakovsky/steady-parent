# Working From Home With Kids

Source: https://www.peacefulparenthappykids.com/read/working-home-with-kids

## Key Concepts

### The Core Challenge
- Children at home during breaks need adult help structuring their time
- Begging and threatening children to be quiet does not work
- Most productive work with young children happens after bedtime
- Working at home while responsible for young children is inherently difficult

### Eight Strategies for Working at Home

#### 1. Fill Your Child's Cup Before Working
- Connect with children before asking them to play independently
- Children without usual routines and friends need more adult support
- Focus on connection first, then engage them in activities they love
- Independent play times can gradually stretch with consistent practice
- Expect interaction during "on-duty" periods with children

#### 2. Manage Workplace Expectations
- Communicate proactively with supervisors about flexibility needs
- Reduce stress by not over-promising deliverables
- Answer communications promptly while setting realistic timelines
- Brief children about meetings in advance with age-appropriate guidance

#### 3. Prepare Sensory Activities
- Stock interesting activities in advance
- Sensory bags and boxes provide engaging independent options

#### 4. Facilitate Independent Play
- Many children struggle with independent play due to:
  - Overly structured lives
  - Screen dependency for managing free time
  - Adult interruption of play from infancy
- Retraining takes time but is achievable

**Daily Practice Technique:**
- Before free play/work time, spend 10 minutes watching your child play
- Set a timer and announce the purpose
- Turn off phone, ensure other children are occupied
- Consciously pour loving attention into your child
- Comment without judging, teaching, or making suggestions
- Use descriptive language about their actions
- Empathize with their excitement

**Benefits:**
- Fills child's emotional cup
- Validates their play as valuable
- Leads to deeper, more sustained play over time

#### 5. Use Screens Strategically
- Screen time as occasional babysitter is acceptable during work needs
- Create schedules with specific times for screen use
- Align screen time with work meeting times
- Choose enriching screen activities:
  - Video calls with grandparents (remote babysitting)
  - Virtual playdates with friends
  - Virtual tours of parks or museums
  - Online book clubs (requires some supervision)
  - Educational games and apps
  - Art tutorials and creative activities
- Invest time upfront preparing activities to buy productive time later

#### 6. Coach Children and Provide Incentives
- Set up activities before calls
- Use visual timers children can see
- Establish protocols for emergencies (waving from doorway)
- Review potential problems and solutions in advance
- Define what counts as an emergency
- Promise enjoyable activities after work time ends

**Post-Call Follow-Up:**
- Give lavish attention after successful independent periods
- Acknowledge their inner strength and self-discipline
- Engage in fun, active play together
- Make the effort worthwhile for the child

**Building Skills:**
- Start with short periods
- Expect occasional interruptions (warn colleagues)
- Each success builds inner resources and self-discipline
- Children develop deeper focus capacity over time

#### 7. Enroll Older Children as Helpers
- Summer break is appropriate time for family members to pitch in
- Provide clear guidelines for:
  - Guiding younger sibling's behavior appropriately
  - Handling specific situations
  - When to interrupt parent
- Compensate with appreciation, special privileges, or increased allowance

#### 8. Trade Shifts with Another Adult
- If two adults are home, alternate on-duty periods
- Schedule concentrated work during off-duty times
- Save interruptible routine work for on-duty times
- Consider staggered schedules (early morning/late evening)
- Communicate schedule to partner to avoid conflicts (e.g., roughhousing during calls)

## Core Principles

1. **Connection precedes cooperation** - Children need emotional cups filled before they can play independently
2. **Realistic expectations matter** - Accept that capacity will be reduced during this time
3. **Preparation enables success** - Investing time in setup yields productive work time
4. **Skill-building is gradual** - Independent play and self-discipline develop over time with practice
5. **Reciprocity motivates children** - Post-work fun makes the effort worthwhile for them
6. **Flexibility is essential** - Things will go wrong; plan for interruptions

## Practical Insights

- Independent play is a learned skill that many children lack due to structured lives and screen habits
- The "admiration technique" (10 minutes of attentive watching) validates play as worthwhile activity
- Screen time is a tool to use strategically, not something to feel guilty about during work needs
- Coaching children through potential scenarios reduces interruptions
- Visual timers help young children understand time boundaries
- Success builds on success - each positive experience strengthens the child's capacity
- Grace and humor about imperfection are essential parenting tools
- Future perspective: Children will remember feeling close during this time, not the conference calls
