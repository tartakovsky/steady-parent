Whether you have a **Toddler, a Preschooler, an Elementary Schooler or a PreTeen** , it can be hard work to come up with ideas to lure your kids away from screens. 
Here's your age-by-age guide to keep children up to the age of twelve busy, with minimal supervision from you. Use these as ideas to get your family brainstorming -- the possibilities are endless!
* * *

  4. Explore ice cubes and pouring toys with water in a baby bathtub on the bathroom or kitchen floor.

### Preschoolers
Preschoolers will love many of the activities on the Toddler List, above. Plus, they're ready for:
  1. Use old cardboard tubes and boxes to build a marble maze.
  2. Make a curving line of dominoes and knock the first one so they fall down in a row
  3. Make an obstacle course in your hall with yarn and tape 
  4. Blindfold your sibling & take them on a tour of your house & yard, then trade places.
  5. Glue popsicle sticks together to make picture frames, decorate.
  6. Build a fort with blankets and pillows.
  7. Bowl in your hallway with soda bottles or toilet paper tubes
  8. Put on music and dance.
  9. Cut out a crown, tape into a circle to fit your head and decorate
  10. Make a drawing of your family.
  11. Use pipe cleaners to make animals
  12. Play at a sand table or with sand in a baby bathtub.
  13. Make a birthday card for the next person you know who is having a birthday
  14. Make paper bag puppets or sock puppets
  15. Cut out pictures from magazines and make a collage
  16. Make a zoo for your stuffed animals.
  17. Make a peanut butter and banana sandwich.
  18. Use masking tape to make a race track for your cars all over your living room
  19. Make placemats (Parents can laminate at the local copy shop)
  20. Set up a shop and be the shop keeper, with play money you make.
  21. Make your room into a rainforest by drawing plants and animals.
  22. Make a sculpture from pretzels and peanut butter.
  23. Do a something kind for someone, in secret.
  24. Make an obstacle course.
  25. Have an indoor "snowball" fight with your sibling, using socks.
  26. Play Simon Says or Mother May I with your sibling
  27. Play Tag or Freeze Tag
  28. Blow bubbles
  29. Mix ivory soap, kleenex and water to make clean clouds on a cookie sheet
  30. Paint sea shells or rocks
  31. Mix liquid hand soap, cornstarch and food coloring into paint and paint the inside of the bathtub.
  32. On a hot day, have a wet sponge toss in the back yard with clean sponges and two buckets of water.
  33. Make a spiderweb in a doorway with painters tape and throw scrunched up newspaper balls at it to see how many you can catch.

### Elementary Schoolers
Elementary Schoolers will love many of the activities on the Preschooler List, above. Plus, they're ready for:

  4. Draw a picture of a desert island with all the things you would want on it.
  5. Cut out paper dolls and costumes for them.

  7. Use a ruler to measure things in your house, recording their length.
  8. Give your dolls or stuffed animals a bubble bath.
  9. Make a boat using a plastic soda bottle base & popsicle sticks (use duct tape) for the top, then float it at the pond.
  10. Write down ten things you love about each person in your family to surprise them.

### Middle Schoolers
Preteens will love many of the activities on the Elementary Schoolers List, above. Plus, they're ready for:
  1. Write a song.
  2. Learn to cook your favorite food.
  3. Make a giraffe, a spaceship or something else out of papier mache.
  4. Learn to play an instrument.
  5. Put on a play.
  6. Play ping pong. (No table? Play against the wall!)
  7. Make jewelry.
  8. Make a video.
  9. Build something from wood.
  10. Record a "letter" to send to someone.
  11. Plant seeds in a window box or start a garden outside.
  12. Put together a terrarium or aquarium.
  13. Make dinner for your family.
  14. Start a Science Journal where you write questions and then record your observations and discoveries.
  15. Learn a language.
  16. Clean out your closet.
  17. Every day for a month, do a random act of kindness.
  18. Read every book by an author you like.
  19. Wash the car.
  20. Hire yourself out to your neighbor as a mother's helper once a week.
  21. Learn a new boardgame.
  22. Draw a comic book.
  23. Get glow-in-the-dark stars and make constellations on your ceiling.
  24. Build a fort or treehouse.
  25. Tie dye a teeshirt.
  26. Write a poem.   
  27. Camp out in your yard.
  28. Organize a bike and vehicle wash for your neighborhood kids (yes, you can social distance.)
  29. Go roller-blading.
  30. Read a book.
  31. Make a list of fun things you can do without a grown-up.
