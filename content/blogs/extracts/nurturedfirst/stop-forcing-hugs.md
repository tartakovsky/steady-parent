# Why You Should Not Force Your Child To Hug Grandparents

**Source:** Nurtured First (Shannon Wassenaar, Registered Psychotherapist)
**Date:** December 12, 2023
**Reviewed by:** Nurtured First's team of child development experts

## Core Problem

**The Scenario:** Grandparents spread arms wide demanding hugs. Child squirms, dodges, hides behind parent. Grandparent lectures about rudeness or disrespect. May forcibly hug the reluctant child while parents stand by uncertain.

**Central Thesis:** Forcing physical touch on children, even with good intentions, negatively impacts the child in both subtle and profound ways by undermining their sense of **bodily autonomy**.

## Harms of Forced Affection

### Undermining Bodily Autonomy

When grandparents or any caregiver force a child to accept hugs or other touches when the child is resisting or saying no, it teaches the child they do not have control over their physical boundaries.

### Negative Consequences

1. **Anxiety and Insecurity:** Children realize they cannot always rely on adults to respect their boundaries
2. **Reduced Boundary Assertion:** Children become less able to assert their boundaries in other situations, leaving them vulnerable to abuse
3. **Confused Internal Signals:** Teaching kids they must accept unwanted affection from relatives sends the message that anyone in the family can touch them even if they protest. This removes their ability to listen to their internal signals about what feels safe and comfortable regarding physical touch.

### Causal Chain

Forced affection from trusted adults (grandparents) leads to confusion about consent leads to inability to distinguish safe from unsafe touch leads to vulnerability to abuse.

## Why Grandparents Force Affection

Understanding grandparent motivations from a place of curiosity allows addressing the issue from understanding rather than judgment.

### Cultural Practice

- Many grandparents grew up when cultural norms around affection and greetings differed from today
- As children, they may have been expected to give hugs to relatives and family friends even when uncomfortable
- Certain social groups open and close gatherings with hugs as customary greeting
- **Recommendation:** Get curious about their customs and culture to put behavior into perspective

### Connection Seeking

- Strong desire to feel connected to grandchild
- Hugging is an attempt to connect, especially if not as close to grandchild as hoped
- If they do not see grandchild often or feel disconnected, forced affection compensates for these feelings
- **Key Insight:** Physical affection might be the only connection tool the grandparent knows how to use
- **Implication:** They need to learn new skills to connect with grandkids in safe and loving ways

### Lack of Awareness

- Grandparents are unaware of negative consequences
- If never told forced hugs cause distress or confusion, their behavior makes sense
- Seems harmless because they have never known it to be harmful
- **Important Distinction:** Understanding their perspective does not make forced affection okay
- **Action Required:** Explain potential harms, assert boundaries, model desired behaviors

## Framework for the Conversation

### Timing

Have the conversation **before** a family gathering. This gives grandparents opportunity to ask questions or process feelings.

### Approach Principles

1. Advocate for child using facts to educate
2. Respect grandparent's perspective
3. Avoid shaming or judgmental tone

### Conversation Structure

**Step 1: Open with Love and Acknowledgment of Intent**

Example scripts:
- "I love that you want to be close to Bella. I know giving hugs is a way you connect with her..."
- "I know when you ask Henry for a hug, it's because you love him!"

**Step 2: Validate and Acknowledge Their Behavior**

Example scripts:
- "I know it's custom for you to greet guests with hugs. I love that affection is important to you!"
- "It makes sense that you ask Kim for a hug when you see her. Your grandparents did the same for you!"

**Step 3: Educate About Impacts**

Example scripts:
- "We are teaching our kids that they are the boss of their body. Receiving a hug without their consent can feel confusing and uncomfortable for them. We ask them if they'd like a hug so they feel safe and in charge of their body."
- "We want our kids to feel cared for, valued, and safe. This means they get to choose when people touch their bodies. When they're forced into physical touch, it can create negative associations with the adults who are trying to hug them. I want our kids to have positive associations with their family members, which is why we ask permission first."

**Step 4: Reinforce Good Intentions**

Acknowledge grandparents have best intentions and want love and bonding. Explain forced touch often has the opposite effect. Frame respecting boundaries as putting grandchild's emotional needs first.

## In-the-Moment Boundary Scripts

For situations when conversation has not happened or grandparents continue forcing affection despite prior discussion:

### Objection: "When you were little, we made you give hugs to your grandparents, and you turned out just fine."

**Response:** "I love that you want my kids to be close to you, like I was with my grandparents. You're a safe and trusted adult, but sometimes unsafe adults try to force affection, so we want our kids to learn to say no."

### Objection: "But, I'm their grandparent, and they should hug me!"

**Response:** "I understand that you love them so much. Is there another way you can show the kids that you love them?"

### Objection: "I don't think it's that big of a deal! Parents are so sensitive now."

**Response:** "I know you did your best for me with the information you had when I was growing up. I am doing the same for my own kids."

## Alternative Ways to Connect

Suggest alternatives after asserting boundaries.

### High Five or Fist Bump

- Playful engagement while respecting personal space
- Example script: "Can I get a high five?" before lightly slapping palms
- Turns asking for consent into a fun interaction

### Secret Signal or Handshake

- Create a secret signal such as a wink or silly face
- Promotes connection without physical contact
- If child is open to handshakes, create a special handshake reserved just for child and grandparent

### Air Hug or Blow a Kiss

- Playful and connected alternative to physical affection
- Make eye contact while tightly crossing arms to mimic a hug
- Allows grandparent to express affection without imposing physical boundary

### Share an Experience

- Take interest in child's hobby or special interest
- Ask questions about their interests
- Invite to share one-on-one experience
- Play games or read books together
- Allows bonding to happen organically through joy of shared experiences

## Conceptual Framework

### The Boss of Their Body

Children are taught they are "the boss of their body." This means:
- They get to choose when people touch their bodies
- Their consent is required before physical contact
- They should feel safe and in charge of their body

### Consent as Safety Training

Teaching consent with safe adults (grandparents) prepares children to:
- Recognize when adults respect or violate boundaries
- Distinguish safe from unsafe adults based on boundary respect
- Say no to unsafe adults who try to force affection

### Positive vs Negative Associations

- Forced physical touch creates **negative associations** with adults attempting to hug
- Respecting boundaries creates **positive associations** with family members
- Goal is positive associations through consent-based affection

## Anti-Patterns

1. **Standing by silently** when grandparents forcibly hug reluctant child
2. **Shaming or judging** grandparents rather than educating with curiosity
3. **Failing to prepare** by having conversation only in the moment
4. **Accepting "you turned out fine"** argument without reframing around safety
5. **Not offering alternatives** leaving grandparents without tools to connect

## Key Principles

1. **Respect bodily autonomy:** Children must have control over their physical boundaries
2. **Understand motivations:** Cultural norms, connection seeking, and lack of awareness drive grandparent behavior
3. **Educate respectfully:** Helpful conversations can enlighten without shaming
4. **Set boundaries with love and firmness:** Acknowledge intentions while asserting importance of consent
5. **Offer alternatives:** High-fives, secret signals, air hugs, shared experiences replace forced hugs while promoting connection
6. **Stand up for children:** Despite pushback, protecting child's comfort, safety, and sense of consent is priority

## Generational Context

- Current generation is learning and using new ways to keep kids safe
- Change may come with pushback from previous generations
- Truth to rest in: protecting and standing up for kids matters
- Modeling consent importance helps children learn their NO truly matters
