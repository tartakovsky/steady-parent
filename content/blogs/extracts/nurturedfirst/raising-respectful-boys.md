# How to Raise Boys Who Are Kind, Empathetic, and Respectful

**Source:** Nurtured First (nurturedfirst.com)
**Author:** Paige Shiels (Registered Early Childhood Educator, Bachelor's in Youth and Children's Studies)
**Date:** November 9, 2021
**Reviewed by:** Nurtured First's team of child development experts

---

## Core Thesis

**Validating boys' emotions does not make them weak.** Instead, validation raises boys to be respectful, kind, strong, and confident. The goal is to raise boys who can be soft, brave, and in tune with their emotions, who have healthy coping mechanisms, and who can regulate their anger and maintain healthy relationships.

---

## The Problem: Toxic Masculinity Messages

### Common Harmful Phrases

- "Boys will be boys"
- "Boys don't cry"
- "Toughen up"
- "You're fine"
- "Man up"
- "It's girly to feel"

### Downstream Consequences of These Messages

1. **Mental health avoidance:** Men are less likely to seek help for mental well-being
2. **Higher suicide rates:** Men have higher rates of suicidal behavior
3. **Silent suffering:** Men struggle in silence with mental health issues
4. **Emotional disconnection:** Men have difficulty feeling tuned into their emotions
5. **Unhealthy coping mechanisms:** Turn to harmful behaviors instead of processing feelings
6. **Therapy resistance:** Less likely to want to attend therapy or get professional help
7. **Intergenerational transmission:** Men who grew up with "big boys don't cry" have difficulty tuning into their own children as parents, perpetuating the cycle

## Domain 1: Raising Respectful Boys

### Anti-Pattern (What to Avoid)
"Oh, they were just rough playing. Boys will be boys!"

### Recommended Approach
"You pushed Sam when he took your toy. It's okay to feel upset, but it's not okay to hit. Let's go check-in and make sure he is okay. Do you think saying sorry will help you both feel better?"

### Causal Chain
- When a child hears "boys will be boys," they interpret this as: "It's okay to push around other people when they do something I don't like"
- The correct message to internalize: "It's okay to feel upset with friends, but it's not okay to hurt them"

### Key Components of the Respectful Response
1. **Name the behavior:** "You pushed Sam"
2. **Identify the trigger:** "when he took your toy"
3. **Validate the emotion:** "It's okay to feel upset"
4. **Set the boundary:** "but it's not okay to hit"
5. **Model empathy:** "Let's go check-in and make sure he is okay"
6. **Teach repair:** "Do you think saying sorry will help you both feel better?"

## Domain 2: Raising Boys Who Reflect

### Anti-Pattern (What to Avoid)
"No wonder that happened! If you had just listened, it wouldn't have happened."

### Recommended Approach
"Tell me more about what happened, and help me understand."

### Underlying Principle
To learn from mistakes, children need to reflect and figure out what they could have done differently. The adult's response to a mistake determines what lesson the child learns.

### Workplace Analogy (Boss Response to Employee Mistake)

**Scenario:** You made a mistake filling out paperwork for an important work order.

| Boss Response | What You Learn |
|---------------|----------------|
| "I can't believe you did that. You really messed it up." | "I suck at this job; I have no idea what I'm doing." |
| "Mistakes happen, that's okay. Let's work together and figure out what happened!" | "When I mess up, I have people who care about me who will help me so I can do better next time!" |

### Goal
Respond to children in a way that communicates:
1. You want to understand what happened
2. You are there to help them do it differently next time

## Domain 3: Raising Strong Boys

### Anti-Pattern (What to Avoid)
"Big boys don't cry. Toughen up!"

### Recommended Approach
"Your feelings matter. It's ok to cry. I'm here with you."

### Counterintuitive Truth About Strength
- **False belief:** The only way to be strong is by "toughening up" and holding in all feelings
- **Reality:** The more we share how we are feeling with those around us, the stronger we can be

### Energy Economics of Emotional Suppression
If we spend all of our energy holding in emotions, we won't have any energy left to stand up and be strong for our loved ones.

### Key Insight
Showing emotions is not weakness. Emotional expression actually enables greater strength and capacity to support others.

## Domain 4: Raising Confident Boys

### Anti-Pattern (What to Avoid)
"I just need to lose this extra roll and drop 5 pounds."

### Recommended Approach
"I'm so grateful for how my body has served me. It's incredible!"

### Mechanism
Children learn body image from watching how parents talk about their own bodies. It's hard for children to be confident and comfortable with their bodies when they hear parents talking negatively about theirs.

### Goal
Children should understand and appreciate how amazing their bodies are.

## Domain 5: Raising Boys Who Feel Safe Sharing Emotions

### Critical Importance
This is emphasized as extremely important for children's long-term wellbeing.

### Risk Factors When Emotional Sharing Is Blocked
Those who have difficulty tuning into and expressing their emotions:
- Turn to unhealthy coping mechanisms
- Are at higher risk for mental health struggles

### Anti-Pattern (What to Avoid)
"I don't want to hear it; you can put on your big boy pants and figure it out."

### Recommended Approach
"You are feeling unsure right now; I know what that's like. Tell me more about what you are worried about. We can figure it out together."

### Key Components of the Safe Response
1. **Name the emotion:** "You are feeling unsure right now"
2. **Normalize through shared experience:** "I know what that's like"
3. **Invite elaboration:** "Tell me more about what you are worried about"
4. **Offer partnership:** "We can figure it out together"

### Timing
Encouraging children to talk about feelings must start from a young age.

## Domain 6: Raising Kind Boys

### Foundational Principle
**Parents are models for children.** Children are constantly watching, listening, and learning from parents. What parents say, children will say. What parents do, children will do.

### Anti-Pattern (What to Avoid)
"They get a turn now because those are the rules."

### Recommended Approach
"I know that you love playing with that toy. See how happy it made your friend to have a turn? That's because you shared. How does it feel knowing that you helped bring them joy?"

### Key Components of the Kind Response
1. **Acknowledge the child's feelings:** "I know that you love playing with that toy"
2. **Point out the positive impact:** "See how happy it made your friend to have a turn?"
3. **Connect action to outcome:** "That's because you shared"
4. **Invite reflection on the positive feeling:** "How does it feel knowing that you helped bring them joy?"

### Contrast with Rules-Based Approach
- Rules-based ("those are the rules") teaches compliance through external authority
- Empathy-based approach teaches kindness through understanding the emotional impact on others

## Summary of Replace/With Patterns

| Domain | Replace (Anti-Pattern) | With (Recommended) |
|--------|------------------------|-------------------|
| Respect | "Boys will be boys!" | Name behavior, validate feeling, set boundary, model repair |
| Reflection | "If you had just listened..." | "Tell me more... help me understand" |
| Strength | "Big boys don't cry. Toughen up!" | "Your feelings matter. It's ok to cry. I'm here with you." |
| Confidence | Negative body talk about self | Gratitude for body |
| Emotional safety | "Put on your big boy pants and figure it out" | Name emotion, normalize, invite sharing, offer partnership |
| Kindness | "Those are the rules" | Highlight emotional impact on others, invite reflection on joy of giving |

## Key Conceptual Hierarchy

1. **Root cause:** Toxic masculinity messages passed down generationally
2. **Mechanism:** These messages teach boys to suppress emotions
3. **Immediate effect:** Difficulty tuning into emotions
4. **Downstream effects:** Unhealthy coping, therapy avoidance, mental health struggles, relationship difficulties
5. **Intergenerational transmission:** Men who can't tune into themselves can't tune into their children
6. **Solution:** Validate emotions instead of dismissing them
7. **Outcome:** Boys who are simultaneously soft, brave, strong, confident, kind, respectful, and emotionally healthy
