# Building Secure Attachment Without Harsh Parenting: Comprehensive Extract

**Source:** Nurtured First (nurturedfirst.com)
**Author:** Jess VanderWier, MA, RP (Registered Psychotherapist, Master's in Counselling Psychology)
**Date:** November 21, 2018
**Reviewed by:** Nurtured First's team of child development experts

---

## Core Thesis

Secure attachment can be achieved through multiple parenting approaches. Attachment parenting methods (breastfeeding, co-sleeping, avoiding sleep training) are not prerequisites for secure attachment. What matters is the quality of emotional responsiveness, not specific practices.

---

## Scientific Definition of Attachment

**Secure attachment** at its scientific core means:
- A baby or child has **deep abiding confidence** that their caregiver will be responsive
- The child experiences a sense of **safety and security**
- The child learns how to **regulate emotions**
- The child has a **secure base** from which they can explore the world

---

## Key Conceptual Distinctions

### Attachment vs. Attachment Parenting

**Critical insight:** Attachment (the psychological bond) and attachment parenting (a specific parenting philosophy emphasizing breastfeeding, co-sleeping, babywearing) are **not mutually exclusive**.

- Breastfeeding and secure attachment are not mutually exclusive
- Co-sleeping and secure attachment are not mutually exclusive
- A child can be formula-fed and sleep-trained while still developing secure attachment

### Attachment Simplified

In the simplest form, **attachment means love**:
- Loving your child best
- Meeting their needs
- Showing up for them every day

## Practical Methods for Building Secure Attachment

The author provides five concrete strategies that foster secure attachment:

1. **Physical and emotional connection**
   - Cuddles
   - Eye contact
   - Undivided attention
   - Play

2. **Emotional validation and co-regulation**
   - Allow the child to experience a full range of emotions
   - Talk through emotions with the child
   - Let the child know they can be happy, mad, sad, and frustrated with the parent present
   - The parent's presence during emotional experiences is what matters

3. **Expanding the child's circle of trust**
   - Allow time with trusted family and friends
   - Purpose: teach the child how to trust others in their life
   - This builds relational skills beyond the primary caregiver

4. **Emotional modeling by parents**
   - Control your own emotions in front of the child
   - Model talking out difficult emotions
   - This begins early in the child's life

5. **Supported exploration and autonomy**
   - Allow exploration without hovering
   - Let the child understand natural consequences
   - Maintain consistent presence so the child knows the parent will be there when exploration is complete
   - Balance between freedom and secure base

## Anti-Patterns and Warnings

### The Harmful Rigidity of Attachment Parenting Dogma

**Warning:** If attachment parenting practices are followed to the point where no one is happy and parental mental health is impacted, then the approach is not working.

**The author's personal experience:**
- When weaning baby to bottle, was told it would impact attachment and bonding
- When sleep training, was told the baby would become less attached and bonding ability would be ruined "for life"
- Multiple people made these claims without knowing the impact of sleep deprivation on the mother
- In reality, breastfeeding (when not working) and not sleep training were **ruining the bond**
- These practices were making the mother "cringe around my baby"
- They were making the mother "not love being a mother"

**Key insight:** Practices that damage parental wellbeing can paradoxically damage attachment more than the "non-attachment-parenting" alternatives.

### The Problem with Mommy Shaming

The concept of attachment parenting has become "very trendy" with a significant downside:
- Women who cannot breastfeed feel they are failing at attachment
- Women who have to sleep train feel they are failing at attachment
- Women who go back to work feel they are not getting the attachment they should

**The author's stance:** This shaming is counterproductive and not based on attachment science.

---

## Causal Chains

### How Parental Mental Health Affects Attachment

Sleep deprivation and unsustainable practices cause:
- Parental exhaustion and irritability
- Negative associations with the baby ("cringing around my baby")
- Loss of joy in parenting
- Damaged bond and responsiveness
- Therefore: paradoxically worse attachment outcomes than the "non-attachment-parenting" alternatives

### How Secure Attachment Develops

Consistent emotional responsiveness leads to:
- Child's confidence that caregiver will respond
- Sense of safety and security
- Ability to regulate emotions (learned through co-regulation)
- Willingness to explore (secure base effect)
- Ability to trust others (expanded from primary relationship)

## The Author's Framework: Owned Motherhood

**Principle:** Own your motherhood journey without worrying about external judgment

**Components:**
- Recognize each family is walking their own journey
- Different children require different parenting approaches
- What works for one family may not work for another
- Make choices that are right for your specific family
- Be loving and kind to yourself and your children
- Encourage other parents rather than shame them

**The author's identity statement:** "No, I'm not an attachment parent, but I'm raising a securely attached kid."

---

## Summary of Key Takeaways

1. **Secure attachment requires emotional responsiveness**, not specific practices like breastfeeding or co-sleeping

2. **Parental mental health matters** for attachment; unsustainable practices can damage the bond they claim to protect

3. **Five pillars of secure attachment:** physical connection, emotional validation, expanded trust circles, emotional modeling, supported exploration

4. **Reject the false dichotomy** between attachment parenting practices and secure attachment outcomes

5. **Individualized approach:** each family must find what works for them, as children and circumstances differ

6. **Community support over shaming:** encourage parents rather than criticizing their legitimate choices
