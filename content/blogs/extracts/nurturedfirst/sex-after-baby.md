# Postpartum Sex: Overcoming Challenges for Tired New Moms

Source: Nurtured First (Dr. Megan Phillips, Psy.D., guest author; reviewed by Jess VanderWier, MA, RP)

## Overview

This article addresses the common struggles couples face with sexual intimacy after having a baby, providing practical strategies to rebuild a sexual relationship while navigating exhaustion, physical recovery, and emotional disconnection.

## The Six Week Postpartum Milestone

**The six week follow-up visit** serves as the medical checkpoint where an OB or midwife evaluates vaginal healing and determines when sexual intercourse can safely resume.

### Emotional Reality at This Stage

- Many mothers cannot fathom inserting anything into their vagina after childbirth
- Partners are often eager for medical clearance to resume sex
- This creates a **love/hate relationship** with the appointment itself
- Medical clearance does not equal emotional or physical readiness

## Why Couples Struggle with Postpartum Sex

### Physical Pain and Discomfort

**Key insight**: Medical clearance does not guarantee a smooth experience.

Common physical barriers include:
- Vaginal tenderness and soreness
- Pain from tearing or stitches
- General discomfort during penetration

**Recommendations for physical pain**:
1. Invest in **water-based lubrication**
2. Go slowly during intercourse
3. Work with a gentle, supportive partner
4. Maintain consistency in trying (parts fit together again over time)
5. Expect gradual improvement rather than immediate return to pre-baby function

### Exhaustion and Sleep Deprivation

**Core dynamic**: New parents are constantly tired, and by bedtime, sleep becomes the overwhelming priority.

**The sex rut cycle**:
1. Parents are exhausted by the time baby sleeps
2. Sex gets deprioritized on a long to-do list
3. A routine of not having sex becomes established
4. This routine becomes the **status quo**
5. Both partners feel disconnected and less intimate
6. The problem compounds over time if unaddressed

**Warning**: Allowing this "molehill to become a mountain" creates deeper relationship issues.

## Step-by-Step Framework for Reconnection

### Step 1: Talk About the Absence of Sex

**The elephant in the room problem**: The longer couples avoid discussing their lack of sex, the harder it becomes to address.

**Why conversations fail**:
- Both parties become defensive
- Partners may go on the attack
- Blame gets assigned where no one is truly at fault

**Recommended approach**:
- Focus on **what you like about sex** rather than what is lacking
- Discuss what you **look forward to** instead of complaints
- Keep the conversation positive to avoid defensive reactions and hurt feelings
- Use this discussion to transition into making a plan

### Step 2: Schedule Sex

**Core principle**: Intentionally schedule a specific day/time for sexual activity.

**Implementation**:
- Mark it on the calendar in ink or set a phone reminder
- Commit to sticking with the scheduled time
- Treat it as a non-negotiable appointment

**Setting appropriate expectations**:

| Phase | Goal | Expectations |
|-------|------|--------------|
| Initial attempts | Simply have sex at all | "Low expectations sex" with no bells and whistles |
| Early weeks | Build consistency | Basics only, anything extra is a bonus |
| After routine established | Mix things up | Gradually reintroduce spontaneity and romance |

**The workout analogy**: Starting a workout routine begins with walks, not 5-mile runs. Sexual reconnection follows the same progressive principle.

**Reframe the concern**: While scheduling sex may seem too formulaic, it effectively jumpstarts intimacy and rebuilds routine.

## When Self-Help Strategies Are Insufficient

### Professional Support Option

**Sex therapists** specialize in helping couples work through sexual ruts.

Characteristics of sex therapy:
- Available nationwide
- Professionals are approachable and non-judgmental
- May be necessary when DIY approaches fail
- Can help both partners feel good again

## Key Mindset Reframes

### Normalizing the Learning Curve

**Fundamental truth**: You have never had a sex life with a baby in the picture before.

Implications:
- A learning curve is expected and normal
- Adjustment takes time and effort
- Sex CAN become enjoyable and regular again
- Active work on the relationship is required

### Reassurance Points

- Physical parts will eventually fit together as they did before
- Former rhythm can be restored with patience
- Routine is achievable even with a baby around
- Both partners can feel connected and intimate again

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

1. **Avoiding the conversation**: Letting the lack of sex remain unaddressed
2. **Assigning blame**: Making the situation about fault rather than circumstance
3. **Waiting for spontaneity**: Expecting sex to happen naturally when exhausted
4. **All-or-nothing thinking**: Believing sex must be perfect or elaborate from the start
5. **Rushing physical intimacy**: Not allowing adequate time for vaginal healing
6. **Skipping lubrication**: Proceeding without proper physical preparation
7. **Suffering in silence**: Not seeking professional help when needed

## Causal Chains

**Physical recovery chain**: Birth trauma → vaginal tenderness → painful sex → avoidance → longer gaps → more anxiety about resuming

**Exhaustion chain**: Baby care demands → sleep deprivation → prioritizing rest → skipping sex → routine of abstinence → disconnection → harder to restart

**Positive intervention chain**: Acknowledge issue → positive conversation → schedule sex → low-pressure attempts → gradual rebuilding → restored intimacy → routine reestablished
