# Knowledge Extract: Toddler Throwing Behavior

## Source
- **Original Article**: 14 month old throwing everything!
- **URL**: https://www.peacefulparenthappykids.com/read/14-month-old-throwing-everything
- **Author**: Dr. Laura Markham

## Key Concepts

### Developmental Understanding
- **Throwing is normal toddler behavior**: Toddlers throw when happy, mad, or finished eating - it's a developmental stage, not defiance
- **Age range**: Behavior typically starts around 14 months and can continue through age 2+
- **Cognitive limitations**: Young toddlers don't fully understand complex concepts like danger or harm, even when told

### Effective Strategies

1. **Provide sanctioned throwing outlets**
   - Ball pits for indoor throwing practice
   - Outdoor ball throwing
   - Throwing soft toys (e.g., stuffed animals over a bannister)
   - Daily dedicated "throwing time" to meet the developmental need

2. **Proactive intervention at meals**
   - Sit with the child during meals
   - Remove food before throwing occurs (anticipate when they're finished)
   - Accept that the child may get upset when throwing is prevented - that's okay

3. **Clear, simple communication**
   - Distinguish what CAN be thrown (soft toys, balls) vs. what CANNOT (hard items, food)
   - Use simple explanations: "OUCH! Remotes are not for throwing"
   - Keep language appropriate to developmental level

4. **Environmental management**
   - Move throwable items out of reach
   - Accept some mess is inevitable

### Emotional Approach
- **Stay patient**: Maintaining patience preserves your influence over the child
- **Empathize**: Acknowledge the child's desire to throw ("I know you love to throw")
- **Set limits calmly**: It's okay for the child to get mad or cry when limits are set
- **Maintain humor and perspective**: This stage will pass and be replaced by new challenges

### Key Insight
The parent's job is to stay patient enough through challenging developmental stages that the child remains open to their influence. Children understand more each day, and consistent, empathetic limit-setting eventually works.

## Practical Takeaways

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Provide lots of appropriate throwing opportunities daily | Expect verbal explanations alone to stop the behavior |
| Sit with child at meals and intervene before throwing | React with frustration (damages relationship) |
| Use simple, clear language about limits | Assume child is being defiant |
| Accept child's upset feelings about limits | Give up on setting limits |
| Keep sense of humor about the phase | Expect complex reasoning to work with toddlers |
